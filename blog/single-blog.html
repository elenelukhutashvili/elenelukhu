Overcoming Imposter Syndrome as a New Developer

Published February 10, 2025

Today, I want to open up about a topic that hits close to home for many of us in tech—imposter syndrome. If you’ve ever felt like you don’t belong or you’re not "good enough" as a developer, you’re definitely not alone.

What is Imposter Syndrome?

Imposter syndrome is that nagging feeling that you’re somehow faking it and that everyone else knows more than you. In a fast-moving field like software development, it’s easy to compare yourself to others and think you come up short.

"I have written eleven books, but each time I think, 'Uh oh, they’re going to find out now. I’ve run a games on everybody.'" — Maya Angelou

Why it Happens in Tech

  1. Constant Innovation: New tools, frameworks, and languages launch daily.
  2. High Expectations: Tech fields move fast, making mastery a moving target.

Strategies to Cope

  • Keep a "Wins" Journal: Track your daily achievements.
  • Seek Feedback: Learn from seniors without fear.
